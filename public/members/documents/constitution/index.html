Constitution of Furrific Ltd

We, the members of Furrific, establish this constitution to govern our operations and uphold our shared values within the furry community. This constitution embodies inclusivity, transparency, and collective governance, fostering a vibrant and welcoming furry community. This constitution is in addition to the articles of association using the devolved powers granted to it by the directors.

1. Name

1.1. The name of the company is “Furrific Ltd”, hereafter referred to as “the organisation.”

2. Purpose

2.1. The purpose of the organisation shall be to provide a platform for its members to engage in social, recreational, and educational activities pertaining to the furry fandom whilst fostering a sense of community and camaraderie among its members.

3. Membership

3.1. Membership in the organisation shall be open to individuals who meet the membership criteria as determined by the organisation’s governing body.
3.2. Annual membership fee, set by the committee.
3.3. Prospective members shall submit a membership application which will require nomination and seconding by existing members, in addition to any required dues or fees as determined by the organisation.
3.4. New members will be subject to vetting by the membership body.
3.5. The directors may revoke or suspend membership for violations of the organisation’s rules, conduct deemed detrimental to the organisation’s reputation or behaviour threatening to the welfare of other members.
3.6. Members must be over 18 years old.
3.7. Members should be considered to be of good moral standing.
3.8. The committee may limit the number of memberships as deemed necessary.
3.9. A new member will be required to wait for an opening if this limit is exceeded.

4. Governance

4.1. Board of Directors

4.1.1. Responsible for legal matters and holds veto power over decisions with legal implications.
4.1.2. Directors must have a clean legal record and automatically hold a position on the committee.
4.1.3. A minimum of three directors are required to prevent stalemate, vacancies are filled promptly through AGM or EGM.
4.1.4. Complimentary membership renewals are granted after three years of service.
4.1.5. Are governed by the articles of association & legal obligations.
4.1.6. Hold de facto positions on committees.

4.2. Committee

4.2.1. Composed of directors board plus at least 2 additional members.
4.2.2. Responsible for overall management and operation of the organisation including setting policies, approving budgets and organising events.
4.2.3. Additional members elected at the AGM.
4.2.4. The size of the committee is decided by directors at the AGM.
4.2.5. Committee may vote to co-opt up to 3 additional members or up to 1% of membership whichever is greater.
4.2.6. Provides strategic guidance and ensures compliance with governing documents.
4.2.7. Committee members shall serve a term of 1 year from date of appointment, with an allowance up to a maximum of 2 years if circumstances require. Committee members may only serve additional years following re-election by members or co-opted by the current committee.
4.2.8. Committee members may be removed for misconduct or neglect of duties by a two-thirds majority vote of the membership.
4.2.9. Responsible for formation of officers and providing support to them.
4.2.10. Should document processes & contacts.
4.2.11. A quorum is achieved when more than half of committee members inc. directors are present.
4.2.12. Resolutions by committee are conducted by instant run-off (alternative vote) with at least two-thirds (66%) majority required to pass.
4.2.13. 3 members of the committee are required to act as chair, secretary & treasurer. These positions are collectively referred to as the committee coordinators.

4.2.14. Committee Chair

4.2.14.1. A primary representative of the organisation, overseeing all its objectives.
4.2.14.2. Chairs all committee meetings ensuring they run smoothly and in an orderly manner.
4.2.14.3. Leads on key decision-making, ensuring all perspectives within the organisation are considered.
4.2.14.4. Acts as a mediator for disputes or conflicts, aiming to maintain a welcoming atmosphere for all members.
4.2.14.5. Develops and drives the long-term vision for the organisation, considering future events, collaborations and community involvement.
4.2.14.6. Ensures that the committee members fulfil their duties and supports them in their roles as needed.

4.2.15. Secretary

4.2.15.1. Organises and documents meetings, including preparing agendas, sending invites, taking minutes and distributing them.
4.2.15.2. Overseas membership applications, renewals and maintains an accurate database of members, ensuring records are up to date.
4.2.15.3. Primary point of communication for members, handling inquiries and distributing announcements and updates.
4.2.15.4. Keeps accurate records of activities, decisions and communications, ensuring they are accessible to the committee.
4.2.15.5. Assists in the planning and organisation of events, helping coordinate logistics and promotions.

4.2.16. Treasurer

4.2.16.1. Oversees the organisation's finances, including budgeting, monitoring expenses and ensuring financial stability.
4.2.16.2. Keep detailed financial records, including income from both member and non-member activities, manage receipts, invoices and payment records.
4.2.16.3. Prepare financial reports for the committee and members, including annual statements, forecasts and balance sheets.
4.2.16.4. Manage the collection of membership payments, ensuring payments are on time and follow up on any late payments.
4.2.16.5. Identifies opportunities for fundraising, sponsorships, and grants to support the organisation's objectives, and manages funds raised through events or other initiatives.
4.2.16.6. Ensures compliance with financial regulations, including proper handling of taxable and non-taxable income, in line with mutual trading rules.

4.3. Team Leads

4.3.1. Appointed by the committee from membership, capable non-committee members are preferred.
4.3.2. Perform tasks delegated by the committee.
4.3.3. Leads may recruit crew/volunteers from membership into their teams where applicable.
4.3.4. Leads serve for a term of 1 year, up to a maximum of 2 years if circumstances require. May be reappointed for additional terms by the committee.
4.3.5. Leads may be removed from positions for misconduct or neglect of duties by a two-thirds majority vote of the committee.

4.4. Fellows

4.4.1. Recruited by leads to assist in their tasks & projects.
4.4.2. Managed directly by leads and report back to them.

5. Meetings

5.1. The committee shall hold regular meetings at least 3 times per year.
5.2. Notice of the meetings shall be provided to all applicable members at least 14 days in advance.
5.3. All meetings shall be conducted according to procedures ensuring order is maintained and all members have an opportunity to participate.

5.4. Voting

5.4.1. Proxy voting is permitted before meetings, members are notified seven days in advance.
5.4.2. Candidate proposals are distributed no less than seven days before elections.
5.4.3. Any member with more than 1 year of membership is entitled to participate in organisation-wide votes.
5.4.4. Alternative vote a.k.a. Instant run-off to be used where there are greater than 2 options available. Rounds continue until a greater than 50% majority is achieved.

5.5. AGM

5.5.1. AGMs are held once per year, ideally within 14 months of the previous meeting where possible. But no more than 24 months.
5.5.2. Directors, chair, secretary & treasurer to submit reports, and additional reports provided by leads where applicable.
5.5.3. The existing committee is dissolved, leads & fellows are relieved of duties.
5.5.4. New committee members are elected by members as follows:
5.5.4.1. Candidates read their proposals.
5.5.4.2. Members vote for candidates through instant runoff a.k.a. Alternative vote.
5.5.5. Committee appoints leads at the first meeting proceeding the AGM.

5.6. EGM

5.6.1. Called by directors or committee secretary upon request.
5.6.2. May be called to resolve matters that can’t wait until the next AGM.
5.6.3. The committee is not dissolved, leads & fellows retain status unless resolved resolutions require.

6. Events

6.1. Organised by committee with support from leads & fellows.
6.2. The committee is responsible for appointing appropriate leads to ensure smooth running of the event.
6.3. To be for the benefit of the majority of members.
6.4. Should be self-funding.

6.5. On-duty Officer Role

6.5.1. Definition and Appointment

6.5.1.1. The On-Duty Officer is a temporary position assigned during the organisation's events to ensure smooth and consistent oversight of event operations.
6.5.1.2. The committee shall appoint an On-Duty Officer for each event or delegate the role in rotation among qualified individuals as designated by the committee.

6.5.2. Authority and Responsibilities

6.5.2.1. The On-Duty Officer shall have the authority to make immediate operational decisions during the organisation’s events in response to urgent matters, safety issues, and general event flow to ensure a secure and enjoyable experience for all members.
6.5.2.2. This role includes overseeing activities, coordinating between the organisation’s event team and members, and addressing unforeseen issues or emergencies.

6.5.3. Limitations of Authority

6.5.3.1. While empowered to make necessary decisions for event continuity, the On-Duty Officer must act in the best interest of the current event, the organisation, plus its members and in accordance with the organisation’s policies and guidelines.
6.5.3.2. Major decisions affecting the organisation’s policies, finances, or constitutional matters require consultation with the committee or the directors if practical within the time frame.

6.5.4. Accountability

6.5.4.1. The On-Duty Officer must document all significant actions taken and decisions made in a report, to be presented to the committee and directors after the event for review.
6.5.4.2. Any actions that exceed the scope of their authority or contravene the organisation’s policies may be subject to committee review and potential corrective actions.

7. Finances

7.1. The organisation shall maintain accurate financial records and operate within a budget approved by the committee.
7.2. Dues and fees shall be determined by the committee.
7.3. The treasurer shall be responsible for collecting dues, paying expenses and providing financial reports to the membership.
7.4. Members may claim for expenses no later than 6 months after the date of receipt. Committee approval is otherwise required.
7.5. Treasurer to provide guidance on expenses which require additional approval.
7.6. All bank accounts should where possible require 2 or more signatories to approve any outgoing payment.
7.7. All directors have access to bank accounts in addition where possible to the treasurer & secretary.
7.8. Accounts should be maintained using a double or triple-entry system.
7.9. Treasurer may select the organisation used for managing the accounts providing it complies with the above.

8. Amendments

8.1. This constitution may be amended by a two-thirds (66%) majority vote by the membership at an AGM or EGM.
8.2. Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing to the secretary and distributed to all members at least 7 days in advance of the meeting.

9. Dissolution

9.1. In the event of the dissolution of the organisation, any remaining assets after liabilities shall be returned to the membership.

10. Ratification

10.1. This constitution shall take effect upon approval by a two-thirds majority vote of the membership.
